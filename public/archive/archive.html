CS:GO Roundtime analysis

  Posted on September, 2017  |  TL;DR
body {text-align: justify} The idea of this analysis is to study in depth the differences between the play styles of CS regions by using solely statistical methods. Not a single demo has been watched in order to make this analysis. The main variable focused on is the length of the rounds won. The idea is to see what kind of rounds the teams are winning on either side. Of course, as there are always to teams in the game, the data set would be biased if I used regional data (such as Esea pro league’s regular season). Due to this reason only data from recent major tournaments is used. Multiple factors play in to winning a game in sports, be it a traditional football (soccer) or esports. Of course, in soccer a winner is decided by the amount of goals scored but that’s not the entire story of the game. Things happen that allow us to forecast winner before the game is even past half time. With talking of football I’m going out of my depth but I’m fairly certain, that the team that manages to have a higher possession of the ball wins more often. This is, as it’s only logical that having a ball generates scoring opportunites which generate goals. The fact that we are confident that on first half other team was obviously better at controlling should forecast that the team is also better at controlling the ball on latter half, and thus more likely to score.
